My model relies primarily on prosodic features extracted from the strictly causal 1.5-second window immediately preceding a pause. The strongest signals used are the energy slope (RMS) and the pitch slope (F0), which mathematically capture the natural trailing-off of human speech at the end of a turn. It also utilizes the mean energy, mean pitch, and total speech duration to contextualize the pause. The model still fails on users who naturally trail off or drop their volume mid-thought (e.g., while searching for a word), as this mimics end-of-turn prosody. It also struggles when heavy background noise obscures the pitch tracker, resulting in flat or missing F0 slopes. With one more day, I would replace the Random Forest with a small PyTorch GRU or LSTM to process sequential frame-by-frame features rather than relying on aggregated slopes over a fixed window. I would also implement basic noise-reduction preprocessing to clean the audio before extracting the pitch contour.
