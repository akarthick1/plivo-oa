For this model, I focused heavily on pitch and energy slopes from the 1.5 seconds right before a pause. When people finish speaking, their voice naturally drops in volume and pitch, and the model picks up on this "trailing off" effect really well. The main issue right now is false positives when a user naturally mumbles or trails off while trying to remember a word, because it looks exactly like an end-of-turn to the model. Heavy background noise also causes problems because it completely scrambles the pitch tracker. If I had another day, I'd drop the Random Forest and build a small PyTorch LSTM to process the audio frame-by-frame instead of just averaging a fixed window. I'd also add some basic noise-reduction preprocessing to clean up the audio before trying to extract the pitch.
